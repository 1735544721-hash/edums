Abstract

Against the background of normalized digital governance and routine teaching quality evaluation in higher education, university academic affairs span multiple links such as classes, courses, course offering plans, timetables, and teaching incident handling; however, current processes suffer from dispersed data, inconsistent business standards, reliance on multiple systems for scheduling and queries, and a lack of closed-loop incident handling, which makes it difficult for teachers and administrators to complete plan arrangement, statistics, and notice publishing on a single platform and weakens the traceability and oversight of teaching data. To address these issues, this paper builds the backend of an academic administration system with Java 17 and Spring Boot 3.2.8, adopts MyBatis-Plus and MySQL to form a unified data model and a paginated query mechanism, and establishes a business loop covering administrator login and teaching configuration, teacher registration and profile maintenance, class and course management, course offering plans and timetable arrangement, teaching incident records, notice and announcement management, and homepage statistics, with OpenAPI/Swagger API documentation to support system integration and extension, thereby improving data consistency, scheduling and query efficiency, process traceability, and the timeliness of management decisions, and providing technical support for standardizing university academic processes and teaching quality governance.

Keywords: academic administration; course scheduling; Spring Boot; MySQL
